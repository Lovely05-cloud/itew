About Laguna

Discover the rich history, vibrant culture, and natural wonders of Laguna Province

Welcome to Laguna!

Nestled between mountains and the country's largest lake, Laguna is a province where history, nature, and culture blend beautifully. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or a taste of authentic Filipino heritage, Laguna has something for everyone.

Did You Know?

Largest Lake

Laguna de Bay, bordering the province, is the largest lake in the Philippines.

Hot Springs

Laguna is famous for its natural hot springs, especially in Los Baños and Pansol.

Birthplace of Rizal

National hero Dr. Jose Rizal was born in Calamba, Laguna.

Lush Forests

Home to Mount Makiling and enchanting rainforests teeming with wildlife.

Why Visit Laguna?

Natural Wonders

Explore waterfalls, lakes, hot springs, and scenic mountains perfect for adventure and relaxation.

Delicious Cuisine

Taste local delicacies like buko pie, espasol, and more in Laguna's vibrant food scene.

Colorful Festivals

History of Laguna

Laguna's story is as deep as its lake. From pre-colonial settlements to Spanish missions, the province has played a vital role in Philippine history. It was here that Dr. Jose Rizal, the country's national hero, was born and inspired to fight for freedom. Today, Laguna's towns are dotted with centuries-old churches, ancestral houses, and monuments that echo its storied past.

Geography & Climate

Laguna is cradled by the Sierra Madre mountains and Mount Makiling, with Laguna de Bay to the north. Its landscape is a tapestry of lush forests, rolling hills, and fertile plains. The climate is warm and tropical, with a refreshing breeze from the lake and mountains—perfect for outdoor adventures year-round.

Culture & Heritage

Laguna is a cradle of Filipino culture. The province is renowned for its vibrant festivals, folk arts, and crafts like woodcarving and embroidery. Local traditions are celebrated with music, dance, and colorful parades, while its culinary heritage delights visitors with unique flavors and treats.

Economy

Laguna's economy is a blend of tradition and innovation. Agriculture remains strong, with rice, coconut, and coffee as mainstays. The province is also a hub for manufacturing, technology, and tourism, making it one of the most dynamic regions in the country.

Notable People

  • Dr. Jose Rizal – National Hero of the Philippines
  • Paciano Rizal – Revolutionary General
  • Teodoro Kalaw – Writer and Statesman
  • Fernando Poe Jr. – National Artist for Film
